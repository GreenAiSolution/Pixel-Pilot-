# Pixel Pilot — Prime Cold Call Script
### The autonomous media buyer. Built to book the flight.

**Who you're calling:** DTC / e-commerce founders, CMOs, and heads of growth running paid ads on Meta, Google or TikTok (Shopify brands, ideally $50k–$500k/mo in revenue or spend).

**The one goal of the call:** Book a 20-minute "Flight Deck" demo — OR trigger a free Zero-to-Live launch on one product URL. You are NOT selling the whole platform on the phone. You are selling the next meeting.

**Tonality rules (this matters more than the words):**
- Calm, low, and unhurried. Downward inflection at the end of sentences — you're a peer, not a pitchman.
- Smile while you talk. It's audible.
- Slow down. Silence is your friend — after you ask a question, shut up.
- You already have clients. You're not desperate; you're offering them a seat.

---

## 1. THE OPENER (Pattern interrupt + permission)

> "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] over at Pixel Pilot — I know I'm calling you cold, so I'll be quick. Can I give you thirty seconds and you tell me if it's worth another minute?"

*(Wait. Let them say "sure" or "go ahead." The permission ask disarms the reflex to hang up.)*

**Why it works:** Naming the cold call kills the guard. Asking permission gives them control, which makes them lean in. The 30-second frame promises brevity.

---

## 2. THE REASON (Problem-first, not product-first)

> "So — most brands I talk to that are spending real money on Meta and Google have the same quiet problem: the ad platforms optimize to *their* revenue, not your profit. You're bidding to ROAS while returns, COGS and refunds eat the actual margin — and no one's watching the account at 2am when a winner's about to scale or a loser's burning budget.
>
> And half the battle is the ads themselves — creative fatigues, production is slow, and you're stuck A/B testing with real spend. Pixel Pilot doesn't just *buy* the media — it *builds the ads too*: scroll-stopping reels and stills forged automatically, refreshed the moment they fatigue.
>
> It's an autonomous media buyer that sits on your account 24/7, wired into Shopify for real profit, makes and moves your ads across Meta, Google and TikTok on its own — hands off the wheel."

*(Pause. Let it land.)*

**Why it works:** You lead with a pain they feel but can't name — "the platform optimizes to its revenue, not yours." That's the sharpest line on the whole website. Use it early.

---

## 3. THE HOOK QUESTION (Get them talking)

> "Quick question so I'm not wasting your time — right now, who's actually flying your ad account day to day? An agency, someone in-house, or is it kind of… you?"

*(Listen. Their answer tells you exactly which objection is coming and which pain to press.)*

Follow with ONE of these based on their answer:

- **If agency:** "Got it — and when they shift budget or refresh creative, is that happening in real time, or are you waiting on a weekly report?"
- **If in-house:** "Makes sense — and is that person able to watch all three channels as one budget, or are they siloed per platform?"
- **If it's them:** "Yeah — so you're the strategist, the buyer, AND the founder. How much of your week is disappearing into the ad manager?"

**Why it works:** You're diagnosing, not pitching. Every answer hands you a specific wound to press.

---

## 4. THE VALUE BRIDGE (Their pain → our instrument)

Pick the ONE that matches what they just told you. Do not list all ten. One sharp point beats a feature dump.

| If they said… | Say this |
|---|---|
| "Our agency is slow / creative gets stale" | "That's the exact gap — our Creative Genome refreshes fatigued ads automatically, within the hour, instead of a three-week production cycle. New scroll-stopping variants ship to the ad set before fatigue kills your ROAS." |
| "Our ads / creative just aren't performing" | "Creative is 80% of paid performance — so we built the whole engine around it. The Genome decodes 10,000+ winning ads into their DNA — hook, pacing, emotional arc — then recombines them into new concepts and forges them as native reels. Your ads stop being guesses." |
| "We can't produce ads fast enough" | "That's the Creative Forge — drop a product, get a cinematic, channel-native ad reel in seconds, then it auto-ships fresh variants as things fatigue. You'll never wait on a production cycle again." |
| "We can't tell what's actually working" | "That's the hardest problem in paid media post-iOS. Our Truth Engine runs incrementality plus media-mix modeling so you finally know what actually drove the sale — and it feeds that back into the buying." |
| "It's too expensive to add headcount" | "Right — Pixel Pilot is priced like the department it replaces, not another $99 tool. One system instead of a buyer, a creative lead, and an analyst." |
| "We're spread across Meta, Google, TikTok" | "That's the Conductor — it treats all three as one portfolio and moves the next dollar to wherever the marginal return is highest, live. No single-platform tool can see that." |
| "We're in a tricky vertical (supplements, med-spa, finance)" | "We actually specialize there — compliance-safe autopilot that scales the niches that get most accounts banned, inside the guardrails." |

---

## 5. THE PROOF PUNCH (Differentiation in one breath)

> "Here's the part that usually gets people to lean in — most tools A/B test with *your* money. Pixel Pilot forges the ads itself, then pre-tests up to 500 variants against synthetic personas modeled on your real customers *before* you spend a dollar, and only launches the predicted winners. It even assembles a different ad for every single viewer — personalized at the impression, not the audience. And the whole thing goes from a single product URL to live campaign in under sixty minutes — the launch agencies bill three weeks for."

**Why it works:** Concrete, almost unbelievable specifics (makes the ads, pre-tests 500, 1:1 per impression, 60 minutes to live). Specificity = credibility.

---

## 5b. THE CREATIVE ANGLE (Lead with this if they live and die by ad creative)

For a lot of DTC brands, *creative is the whole game* — so make it the headline, not a footnote. If the prospect complains about creative, fatigue, or "our ads just don't hit," run this instead of the budget angle:

> "Most brands think their ad problem is a *buying* problem — it's almost always a *creative* problem. So Pixel Pilot is built creative-first:
>
> — **It makes the ads.** Drop a product and the Creative Forge fires out cinematic, channel-native reels in seconds — TikTok, Reels, Shorts, Feed.
> — **It knows *why* ads win.** The Genome has mapped 10,000+ proven ads down to their genes — hook, pacing, framing, emotional arc — and recombines the winners into new concepts. That's a dataset a prompt can't clone.
> — **It never lets creative go stale.** The second an ad fatigues, it forges and ships fresh variants automatically — no three-week production cycle.
> — **It personalizes to the person.** A different ad assembled for every viewer based on time, weather, behavior — 1:1 at the impression.
>
> So you're not just getting a buyer that spends smarter — you're getting an infinite, self-refreshing creative team."

**The line to remember:** *"We don't just buy your ads — we build them, test them, and refresh them on their own."*

---

## 6. THE CLOSE (Ask for the flight, not the sale)

**Primary close — the demo:**

> "I don't want to sell you anything on a cold call. What I'd rather do is show you the Flight Deck live for twenty minutes — you'll see it move budget and forge a creative in real time. Worst case you steal a couple of ideas for your own account. You more of a [Thursday] or [Friday] person this week?"

*(Give two options. Never "are you free?" — always "this or that.")*

**Alternate close — the irresistible offer (use if they're skeptical):**

> "Actually — tell you what. Give me one product URL. I'll have Pixel Pilot run a full zero-to-live flight plan on it — market research, personas, strategy, and the first creative batch — and I'll walk you through what it built. Costs you nothing but the URL. Fair?"

**Why it works:** The demo is low-commitment; the free launch is a value-first trap that's almost rude to refuse. Both advance the deal without asking for money.

---

## 7. OBJECTION HANDLING (The book — memorize these)

**"We already have an agency."**
> "Totally — and I'm not asking you to fire anyone today. Most brands run us alongside for thirty days and let the numbers make the call. If your agency's beating an autonomous buyer wired to real profit, you should absolutely keep them. Want to see the comparison?"

**"We do it in-house."**
> "Smart — so you've got someone good. Question is whether one person can watch three channels 24/7, refresh creative within the hour, AND run incrementality testing. Pixel Pilot doesn't replace the strategist — it gives them a buyer that never sleeps. Worth a look?"

**"AI can't run my ads."**
> "I'd have said the same a year ago. That's exactly why I want to show you live instead of tell you — twenty minutes, you watch it move real budget and explain every decision. If it's smoke, you'll spot it in five. Fair test?"

**"It's too expensive."**
> "Fair — what are you spending on paid media a month right now? … Okay. So the question isn't the retainer, it's whether an extra few points of net margin on [$X] covers it — and we only take a slice of spend, so we don't win unless you do. That's why the demo matters — let me show you the profit math on your numbers."

**"Just send me an email."**
> "Happy to — but honestly the email won't do it justice, it's a visual thing watching budget flow live. Give me twenty minutes and I'll send the recap after so you've got both. [Thursday or Friday]?"

**"I'm not interested."**
> "No worries — can I ask, is it that ads are handled and humming, or just bad timing? … [If handled:] Then you're exactly who'd get the most out of a second opinion that costs you nothing. One URL, I'll show you what it builds, and if it's worse than what you've got, you'll have proof you're already winning."

**"How do I know it won't tank my account / get me banned?"**
> "That's the Compliance Guard — every single asset gets screened against platform policy before it goes live, zero unsafe launches, full audit trail. We're built for the aggressive verticals precisely *because* account health is the whole game."

**"Send me pricing."**
> "Sure — three tiers, starts at $2,500 a month plus a small slice of spend, scales up to full fleet. But pricing only makes sense next to what it manages on *your* account, so let's do the twenty minutes and I'll map the right tier to your spend. When works?"

## 8. THE VOICEMAIL (Keep it short, curiosity-first)

> "Hey [Name], it's [Your Name] at Pixel Pilot. Quick one — we run an autonomous media buyer that optimizes Meta, Google and TikTok to real profit, not platform ROAS. I've got a specific idea for [Company]'s account. I'll shoot you a text too — or call me back at [number]. Talk soon."

*(Follow every voicemail with a one-line text: "Hey [Name] — [Your Name] from Pixel Pilot, just left you a VM. 20 min to show you an autonomous buyer that flies to profit? — worth a look for [Company].")*
