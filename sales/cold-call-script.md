# Pixel Pilot — Cold Call Script (studied to close)

> **The one job of the call:** not to sell the platform — to earn a **20-minute "Flight Plan" demo**.
> You are a confident peer with a contrarian insight, not a vendor reading features.
> **Tonality > words.** Slow down. Lower your pitch. Ask a question, then *shut up*.

---

## 0 · Who you're calling (ICP)
- **DTC founders / heads of growth / ecom leads** spending **$50k–$1M+/mo** on paid.
- **Performance agency owners** (they can white-label Fleet Command).
- **In-house media buyers** drowning in manual work.
- **Bonus:** regulated verticals — supplements, med-spa, finance, crypto, cannabis (services #07 is a wedge).

---

## 1 · The opener — pattern interrupt + permission (10 seconds)
> "Hey [Name], it's [You] with **Pixel Pilot**. Full honesty — this is a cold call. Can I take **30 seconds** to tell you why I called, and if it's not relevant you tell me to get lost? Fair?"

*Why it works: radical candor + micro-commitment disarms the reflex "not interested." Wait for the yes.*

---

## 2 · The reason — the contrarian hook (20 seconds)
> "We build **autonomous media buyers** for brands doing $50k+ a month on Meta, Google and TikTok. The pattern we keep seeing: you're paying the platforms to optimize to **ROAS** — which, post-iOS, is basically a vanity number *they* report — while your **actual profit per customer** is a black box. Curious if that's roughly true on your side, or if you've already cracked it?"

*Now stop. Let them react. Their answer tells you which service to lead with.*

---

## 3 · Discovery — 3 questions that qualify AND agitate
1. **Profit truth:** "When you judge a campaign, do you know the **real profit per customer** after COGS and returns — or is it blended ROAS you're steering by?"
2. **Who flies it:** "Who's making the **daily budget calls** — you, a buyer, an agency? And realistically, how fast do losers actually get killed?"
3. **Pain/urgency:** "What's the thing that would make the next 90 days a win — more volume, better margin, or just getting your time back?"
   - *Regulated vertical? add:* "Ever had an account **restricted or banned**? How'd that go?"

*Take notes. Every pain maps to a service below.*

---

## 4 · The pitch — one big idea, then map pains to the deck
**Lead line (always):**
> "Here's the whole idea: **you don't get a login — you get a media buyer.** It lives in your Slack, works 24/7, and makes the calls *in-platform* — no one clicking buttons."  *(Service 01)*

Then weave **only the 2–3 services that hit their pain.** Don't list all ten — *diagnose*, then prescribe:

| If they said… | You say (service) |
|---|---|
| "We steer by ROAS / margin's fuzzy" | **Profit-Optimized (02):** "It bids to your bank account — wired into Shopify for real COGS, returns and LTV. Meta will never optimize against its own revenue. We do." + **Attribution Truth (08):** "Media-mix modeling + live incrementality cuts the post-iOS fog and feeds the optimizer reality, not platform fiction." |
| "We're siloed per channel" | **Cross-Channel Conductor (03):** "One brain across Meta, Google and TikTok — it moves the next dollar to wherever its marginal return is highest, in real time. No single-platform tool can see that." |
| "No bandwidth / losers linger" | **Autonomous Buyer (01):** "It reallocates budget, kills losers and scales winners 24/7 without waiting on a human." |
| "Creative fatigues / need volume" | **Creative Genome (04):** "We decode 10,000+ winning ads into structural genes and recombine them — a dataset, not a random generator." + **Impression-Level Creative (10):** "A different ad for every viewer — assembled per impression." |
| "We waste spend testing" | **Synthetic Pre-Testing (05):** "Test 500 ads against LLM personas of your real customers *before* you spend $1. Kill the dogs in silico." |
| "We get banned in our vertical" | **Compliance-Safe Autopilot (07):** "We scale the niches that get accounts nuked — writing and optimizing inside policy guardrails and managing account health. Zero surprise bans." |
| "Onboarding/agencies are slow" | **Zero-to-Live (09):** "Point it at a URL, walk away, come back to live ads — market research to live campaign in under an hour." |
| "Why not just hire someone / switch later" | **Data Flywheel (06):** "Every dollar trains a model *private to your account* — the longer it flies the sharper it gets. It's an asset you own, and it compounds." |

*Rule of the deck: name the pain, hand them the fix, tie down — "does that hit the thing you're actually fighting?"*

## 5 · Objection handling — tactical empathy (label → reframe → question)
- **"Just email me something."**
  > "Totally fair — and honestly, a PDF won't do it justice. It sounds like the bar is *don't waste my time*. What if I just **show** you it turn a URL into a live strategy in 20 minutes — and if it's underwhelming you never hear from me again?"
- **"We already have an agency / in-house buyer."**
  > "Good — most of our best clients did too. We're not the person clicking buttons; we're the **brain that makes it 24/7 and steers to profit, not ROAS.** Would your buyer be threatened by that, or thrilled to stop babysitting dashboards?"
- **"What's it cost?"**
  > "Straight up: it's priced against the **media buyer you'd hire, not the SaaS you'd cancel.** Pilot's **$2,500/mo + 8% of spend** for one channel, Squadron's **$6,000 + 6%** for the full mix, Fleet's **$15k + 4%** unlimited. A senior buyer is $120k+ a year and sleeps. Which tier fits depends on your spend — want me to show you the math on a call?"
- **"AI can't really do this / I'm skeptical."**
  > "You *should* be skeptical — most 'AI ad tools' are a prompt box. The difference is the **proprietary creative genome and a model that's private to your account.** Don't take my word — let me show it live and you judge."
- **"Now's not a good time."**
  > "Understood — when it's not a priority, it's usually because the current setup isn't *bleeding* yet. If I could show you the profit you're leaving on the table in 20 minutes, is that worth a Thursday slot, or genuinely not this quarter?"

## 6 · The close — assumptive, low-friction, book the flight
> "Here's what I'd suggest — **no pitch.** Give me a product URL and **20 minutes**, and I'll show you Pixel Pilot go from that URL to a live campaign strategy in under an hour, optimizing to your **real margin.** Worst case, you steal the strategy. Are you better **Tuesday morning or Thursday afternoon**?"

*Double-bind close. Get the calendar hold before you hang up. Send the invite while on the phone.*
